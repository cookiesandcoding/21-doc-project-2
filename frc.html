French Croissant Recipe

  • 500g all-purpose flour
  • 10g salt
  • 80g granulated sugar
  • 10g instant yeast
  • 300ml cold water
  • 250g unsalted butter, chilled
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
  1. Mix flour, salt, sugar, and instant yeast in a large bowl.
  2. Add cold water gradually, mixing until a rough dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for about 10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  4. Shape the dough into a rectangle, wrap in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.
  5. Prepare the butter block by pounding and rolling chilled butter into a square.
  6. Roll out the dough, place butter block in the center, fold dough over butter, and repeat folding and chilling.
  7. Roll out dough, cut into triangles, and shape croissants. Proof and bake until golden brown.

Coq au Vin Recipe

  • 4 chicken thighs
  • 200g bacon, diced
  • 250ml red wine
  • 250ml chicken broth
  • 12-15 pearl onions, peeled
  • 200g mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • Fresh thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  1. In a large skillet, cook diced bacon until crispy. Remove and set aside.
  2. Season chicken thighs with salt and pepper. Brown them in the same skillet until golden. Remove and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, melt butter and sauté pearl onions, mushrooms, and minced garlic until onions are caramelized.
  4. Sprinkle flour over the vegetables and stir well. Cook for 1-2 minutes.
  5. Slowly pour in red wine and chicken broth, stirring constantly to avoid lumps. Bring to a simmer.
  6. Return chicken and bacon to the skillet. Add fresh thyme. Cover and simmer for 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until chicken is cooked through and tender.
  7. Serve hot with crusty bread or over mashed potatoes.

Crème Brûlée Recipe

  • 6 egg yolks
  • 100g granulated sugar, plus extra for caramelizing
  • 500ml heavy cream
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Berries for garnish (optional)
  1. Preheat oven to 300°F (150°C). Place 6 ramekins in a baking dish.
  2. Whisk egg yolks and sugar until pale and creamy.
  3. Heat heavy cream until just simmering. Remove from heat.
  4. Slowly pour hot cream into egg mixture, whisking constantly.
  5. Stir in vanilla extract. Strain mixture through a sieve into a pouring jug.
  6. Pour custard mixture into ramekins. Place in a baking dish with hot water.
  7. Bake for 35-40 minutes until set but slightly wobbly in the center.
  8. Chill for at least 2 hours. Before serving, sprinkle sugar on top and caramelize with a torch.
  9. Garnish with berries and serve.
